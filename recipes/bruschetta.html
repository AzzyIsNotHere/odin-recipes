Greek Bruschetta with Feta

Description

This Greek bruschetta recipe is sure to give your guests a pleasant appetizer, and also makes for a great snack that can be made anytime!

Ingredients

  • 1 (10.5 oz.) french baguette
  • 4 oz. (about a cup) of feta cheese, crumbled
  • 1/2 cup plain greek yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons of chopped fresh basil, divided
  • 1 minced clove of garlic
  • 2 tablespoons of extra virgin olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon of red wine vinegar
  • 1/2 teaspoon of dried oregano leaves
  • 1/2 teaspoon of salt
  • 1/8 teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • 1 1/2 cups chopped tomatoes, seeds removed
  • 1/2 peeled large cucumber, seeded and chopped
  • 1/4 large red onion, diced

Directions

  1. Cut the baguette on a diagonal into ½ to ¾ of an inch slices (makes about 22 slices). Arrange into one layer on a baking sheet. Place under a broiler set to high until the top of the bread is lightly toasted.
  2. Add the feta, yogurt, garlic, and 1 tablespoon of basil to a small bowl and mash together with a fork. Store covered in the refrigerator for up to 8 hours.
  3. In a small bowl, add the olive oil, vinegar, oregano, salt, and pepper. Whisk until well combined. Add the tomatoes, cucumber, red onion, and remaining basil. Stir to combine. Store covered in the refrigerator for up to 8 hours.
  4. Divide the feta mixture between the bread slices. Spread into a thin layer on the toasted side of the bread. Top with the tomato mixture.
